 Personal Life and Work

  Check out his work on and off the big screen. 

Ryan Stiles is a Canadian American actor, comedian, and producer. He was born in Seattle Washington and raised in Vancouver, British Columbia. Stiles began his career in comedy in the late 1970s with the Second City comedy troupe after gaining attention from his various improv and standup comedy work around the Vancouver area. He rose to fame in the 1990s as a cast member of the comedy show “Whose Line Is It Anyways?” Stiles has also appeared in numerous films and TV shows including “The Drew Carey Show” and “Two and a Half Men.” In addition to his acting career Stiles also supports various charitable foundations. He is a regular participant in the Annual Comedy Benefit for Gilda’s Club in Seattle, which helps people with cancer and their families. Stiles also co-founded The Upfront Theatre, a comedy club in Bellingham, Washington, that supports local charities through fundraisers and donations.
